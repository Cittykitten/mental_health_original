Anxiety Management Techniques

Diaphragmatic Breathing

Slow, deep breathing that activates the parasympathetic nervous system to reduce anxiety symptoms.

Steps:

  1. Sit comfortably with one hand on chest, one on belly
  2. Inhale deeply through nose for 4 seconds, feeling belly rise
  3. Hold breath for 2 seconds
  4. Exhale slowly through pursed lips for 6 seconds
  5. Repeat for 5-10 cycles

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Systematically tensing and relaxing muscle groups to reduce physical tension.

Steps:

  1. Start with feet, tense muscles for 5 seconds
  2. Release tension suddenly, notice difference
  3. Work upward through legs, abdomen, arms, etc.
  4. Finish with facial muscles and scalp
  5. Practice daily for best results

Depression Coping Strategies

Behavioral Activation

Increasing engagement with positive activities to counteract depression's inertia.

Steps:

  1. List activities you used to enjoy
  2. Start with smallest, easiest activities
  3. Schedule specific times to do them
  4. Rate mood before and after
  5. Gradually increase activity level

Gratitude Journaling

Focusing on positive experiences to counteract negative bias in depression.

Steps:

  1. Each evening, write 3 good things from your day
  2. Be specific and describe details
  3. Note why each mattered to you
  4. Reflect on how you contributed
  5. Review past entries weekly

Stress Reduction Methods

Time Management

Structured approach to reduce overwhelm from multiple demands.

Steps:

  1. List all tasks and responsibilities
  2. Prioritize using Eisenhower Matrix (urgent/important)
  3. Break large tasks into smaller steps
  4. Schedule focused work periods with breaks
  5. Learn to say no to non-essentials

Mindful Walking

Using movement to ground yourself in the present moment.

Steps:

  1. Walk at natural pace, indoors or outside
  2. Notice sensation of feet touching ground
  3. Observe rhythm of breath with steps
  4. When mind wanders, gently return to sensations
  5. Expand awareness to sounds, sights, smells

Anger Management Techniques

Timeout Procedure

Structured approach to prevent escalation during anger episodes.

Steps:

  1. Recognize early warning signs of anger
  2. Politely excuse yourself from situation
  3. Go to predetermined quiet space
  4. Practice calming techniques for 15-30 minutes
  5. Return only when calm to discuss issue

Cognitive Restructuring

Challenging anger-inducing thoughts with more balanced perspectives.

Steps:

  1. Identify triggering situation
  2. Write down automatic angry thoughts
  3. Challenge distortions (mind reading, labeling, etc.)
  4. Develop alternative interpretations
  5. Rate anger level before and after
